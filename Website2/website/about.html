About me:

My name is Mohamed Abdalla and I recently graduated with a bachelors of science from De Montfort University. My family moved to the UK from Libya in 2014 as my youngest brother had autism, while we were in the UK a second civil war started in Libya and we decided to stay in the UK as it was no longer safe to go back.

My family moved to Luton and I started year 10 and began preparing for my GCSE exams. Whilst doing this I was attending a libyan school in london during the weekend as we werent sure if our stay in the UK was going to be permanent. During my free time I would volunteer for a local charity called FOBE (Friends Of Bright Eyes) which is a charity that organises regular care sessions for children with physical and mental disabilities. I liked to stay active so I joined a local mixed martial arts gym where I had several competetive interclubs and competitions.

In August 2018 my family completed the immigration process to the UK. I applied and got into DMU and decided to study a bachelors of science. During my time in DMU I joined the American football team. It was a great opportunity to work with a team of over 50 people and having an undefeated season.
